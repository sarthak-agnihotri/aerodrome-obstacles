Area Analysis Method

The Area Analysis Method is essential in identifying and evaluating potential obstacles in defined zones around aerodromes. It ensures that any physical obstruction—be it buildings, terrain, or vegetation—is documented and managed for safe aircraft operations.

Key Elements of Area Analysis

  • Definition of analysis zones around the aerodrome (e.g., inner horizontal, conical, approach surfaces)
  • Assessment of natural terrain and its elevation relative to the aerodrome reference point
  • Identification of man-made structures like buildings, towers, and antennas
  • Use of aerial surveys or satellite imagery for mapping and detection
  • Comparison against Obstacle Limitation Surfaces (OLS) as per ICAO Annex 14

Tools Used

Tools such as GIS (Geographic Information Systems), LiDAR data, and high-resolution terrain models are frequently employed to assist in accurate obstacle mapping.

Benefits

  • Improved situational awareness for pilots and air traffic control
  • Enhances compliance with regulatory standards
  • Supports long-term airport development and zoning
