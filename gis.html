Use of GIS Tools

GIS (Geographic Information Systems) empower aerodrome planners and analysts by providing spatial data visualization and mapping that improves safety and decision-making in aviation environments.

Overview

GIS integrates various spatial datasets like elevation models, obstacle databases, land use data, and OLS (Obstacle Limitation Surfaces). These allow stakeholders to assess airspace safety visually and analytically.

Applications in Aviation

  • Evaluating new construction projects near airports
  • Monitoring vegetation encroachment in critical zones
  • Modeling takeoff and landing zones in 3D
  • Assessing penetrations of Obstacle Limitation Surfaces (OLS)
